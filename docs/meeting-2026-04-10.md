## Key Alignments

### 1. Translation is inherently multi-dimensional

Translation is not simply converting text from one language to another. It requires consideration of linguistic, geographic, and cultural aspects. Hiring one or two professional translators at high cost does not solve this — no single translator can cover all these dimensions across multiple languages.

### 2. Source content evolves over time

Original documents (e.g., Daybreak/Discovery devotionals and other materials) are subject to revision and updates over time. When source content changes, re-translating manually each time is impractical and unsustainable.

### 3. TGV is a feedback-driven improvement system, not a one-time translation effort

The goal is **not** to produce 100% quality translations immediately. Instead, TGV aims to build a system that:
- Collects structured feedback on translations
- Organizes feedback by language, culture, and theological accuracy
- Improves translation quality progressively over time

Initial results are encouraging — when Gemini AI is given a brief description of our denomination's doctrinal direction, the Japanese translation quality was notably superior to manual alternatives (e.g., Google Chrome built-in translation, DeepL).

### 4. Closed Beta with feedback collection

The two devotionals (Daily Devotional and Daybreak) currently served on **afc-dev.org** will enter a **closed beta**:
- Limited audience of native-speaker ministers per language
- Feedback UI added to the site so reviewers can submit translation corrections and suggestions directly
- Ministers will be asked to use the site regularly and provide ongoing feedback
- Feedback will be incorporated to continuously improve translation prompts and quality

### 5. Email subscription service

If feasible, add an **email subscription** feature — delivering translated devotionals directly to subscribers' inboxes, similar to how the English devotional is currently distributed via email.

### 6. Agent-driven development — communicate to HQ

This site was developed using **agentic AI coding tools**, resulting in:
- Development cost dramatically lower than traditional SWE team hiring
- Development speed/cadence far exceeding conventional approaches
